CRISP-DM Analytics

Introduction

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a widely adopted data science methodology used to convert raw data into meaningful business insights. It provides a structured, iterative, and business-oriented framework for analytics projects.

Definition and Origin

CRISP-DM was developed in the late 1990s by IBM, Daimler-Benz, SPSS, and NCR to establish a standardized approach to data mining across industries. Its primary objective is to ensure that data analytics projects remain aligned with business goals while allowing flexibility and iteration.

Problem Statement

Watsons Malaysia operates in a highly competitive beauty and personal care market. Maintaining customer loyalty among Gen Z consumers has become increasingly challenging. This study applies the CRISP-DM methodology to identify key factors influencing customer satisfaction and loyalty for data-driven decision-making.

CRISP-DM Methodology Overview

Business Understanding

Focuses on project objectives from a business perspective.

Data Understanding

Initial data collection and exploring the quality of data.

Data Preparation

Cleaning and transforming raw data into a final dataset.

Modeling

Selecting and applying various statistical techniques (e.g., Regression).

Evaluation

Determining if the results actually meet the business goals.

Deployment

Presenting the final report and implementing changes.

Purpose and Applications

CRISP-DM is used to ensure analytics projects produce actionable outcomes aligned with business needs. It is commonly applied in retail analytics, healthcare, finance, marketing, and fraud detection.

Real-World Case Study: Watsons Malaysia

Phase 1 Business Understanding

Clear definition of loyalty-related business challenges.

Phase 2 Data Understanding

Validated dataset of 150 Gen Z respondents.

Phase 3 Data Preparation

Cleaned and structured survey dataset.

Phase 4 Modeling

Regression model identifying key influencing variables.

Phase 5 Evaluation

Model confirmed satisfaction mediates loyalty.

Phase 6 Deployment

Watsons should prioritize competitive pricing and store convenience to improve satisfaction and long-term loyalty among Gen Z consumers.

CRISP-DM Impact

Advantages and Limitations

Advantages

  • Business-oriented
  • Industry neutral
  • Flexible and iterative
  • Widely adopted

Limitations

  • No specific algorithm guidance
  • Time-consuming for small projects
  • Requires domain expertise

Industry Adoption

CRISP-DM is widely used by companies such as IBM, Amazon, Netflix, banks, healthcare institutions, and retail organizations for data-driven decision-making.
